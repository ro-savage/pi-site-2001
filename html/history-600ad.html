Pi: Paradise for number lovers

A history of pi-ness:

 

1600 to 1900

By 1600, the brute force approach of using Archimedes formula was all but given up.

It took too long to discover digits of Pi, so people had to either find a new way of finding the value of Pi or be stuck with only a few digits. Even though I say that 35 digits is a small amount, 10 digits of Pi would be sufficient to give the circumference of the earth to a fraction of an inch. 

   In 1921 a Dutch man named Willebrod Snell found a way to calculate Pi, using this formula he proved van Ceulen’s calculation of Pi to 35 digits correct. Over the next few hundred years, there was a race to see who could develop the best formula to calculate Pi. Eventually by 1853 William Shanks found Pi to it’s 607th digit correctly using an arctangent formula.

Shanks formula: p/4 = =4 arctan(1/5) – arctan(1/70) + arctan(1/99)
