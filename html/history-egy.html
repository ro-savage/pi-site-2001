Pi: Paradise for number lovers

A history of pi-ness:

 

The Egyptians

The Egyptians were the first to record Pi, though they did not have a symbol or name for ‘Pi’, they did record what it’s value was. Pi is written on the Rhind papyrus, it says “Cut off 1/9 of a diameter and construct a square upon the remainder; this has the same area of a circle”.

This implies that that the ratio of the circumference to its diameter is 256/81 or 3.16049….

This calculation is only 2 tenth of a percent off the actual value of Pi.
