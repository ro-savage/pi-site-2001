Calculate Pi,

The way the Egyptians could have

 

What is needed:

1 long piece of rope.

A medium sized stick

A small stick

Something to cut the rope

 

What to do:

Cut the piece of rope about a quarter way down, then tie this piece of rope to the medium stick. Then tie the small stick to the other side of the rope.

Find a soft piece of dirt or sand, and stab the medium stick into the ground, from there pull the rope tight and then walk around in a circular motion, keeping the rope tight, also make sure while you are walking that the small stick is marking the ground. You now have a circle drawn in the ground. Proceed to undo the small stick. Now get your original piece of rope and lay one end of the rope on the perimeter of the circle, then pull the other side to the opposite side of the circle (or as close as you can), make sure the other end of the rope stays where it was first placed. Make the rope as straight as you can and cut it at the edge of the circle. You now have a piece of rope the size of the diameter.

Lay the rope around the perimeter, mark where it starts and ends.

Then lay the rope on the perimeter again, with one end touching one of the marks you have just made. Continue doing this until you see that the rope won’t fit anymore.

Get the last piece of the rope and lay it as you were before, and cut it where it meets up with the first mark you made.

Then lay this new cut piece of rope next to the piece of rope that is the size of the diameter and you should that it is around 1/7 of the other rope’s size.

If done correctly you should see that the diameter goes into the circle 3 1/7 times or 3.1428571… times, which is only 1 hundredth of the actual value of Pi.

Try again with a different sized circle and you will notice that this does not change, no matter the size.
