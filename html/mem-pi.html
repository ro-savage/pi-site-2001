Pi: Paradise for number lovers

Memorizing Pi:

Pi, a number with no end, a number that rarely needs to be calculated to more than 6 digits. But for some reason people all over the world have decided to memorize Pi to thousands of digits. Some people try to memorize Pi by just saying it over and over in their head, which works but is not a very good strategy for remember more than a few digits. Others attempt to memorize it by making each note in a song equal to a number, others (such as myself) use word-length mnemonics in which the number of letters in each word represents a digit of Pi (i.e. How = 3, I = 1, Wish = 4 etc…). I myself have memorized Pi to the 12 digits using this mnemonic “How I wish I could calculate Pi. Granma would die froms granitus.”. Some other good mnemonics are; “How I wish I could calculate Pi” which gives you the digits to 7, ‘How I like a drink, alcoholic of course, after the heavy lectures involving quantum mechanics.” Which give you Pi to 15 digits. These are more than enough digits for most people, even the most precise physicists normally only use Pi to 16 digits (which happens to be the same as most computers.). If you wish to memorize Pi to more than 16 digits, you need to use a different technique or a poem, rhyme etc. Such as this rhyme by an unknown person or persons.

 

“Sir, I bear a rhyme excelling

In mystic force and magic spelling

Celestial sprites elucidate

All my own striving can’t relate.

Or locate they who can cogitate

And so finally terminate.  Finis”

 

At the 31st digit we come to the number ‘0’ which becomes a problem but most people use this method –: 9 letter word = 9, 10 letter word  = 0, 11 letter word = 1 followed by 1 [11] etc)

One of the best ways of remembering an extreme amount of digits, 740 to be exact is to use the method described above with the poem by Mike Keith, “Pie. E.: Near a Raven”.

It is carefully crafted to sound like the infamous poem ‘The Raven’ by Edgar Allan Poe.  Here is a snippet from the poem, Poe. E. Near a Raven. To read the full poem click here.

 

Poe, E

    Near a Raven

Midnights so dreary, tried and weary.

Silently pondering volumes extolling all by-now obsolete lore.

During my rather long nap-the weirdest tap!

An ominous vibrating sound disturbing my chamber’s antedoor.

“This,” I whispered quietly, “I ignore.”

 

Perfectly, the intellect remembers: the chostly fires, a glittering ember.

Inflamed by lightning’s outbursts, windows cast penumbras upon this floor.

 

Sorrowful, as on mistreated, unhappy thoughts I heeded:

That inimitable lesson in elegance-Lenore-

740 digits may seem like a lot but when you consider that Simon Plouffe memorized Pi to 4,096 digits or 2¹² it does not seem so big. He did this by isolating himself in a room, with no lights, smokes, noise, coffee or any other luxuries, and recited them to himself over and over and over, until they started appearing in his head, getting into his mind, his memories, everything. He says that it was mystical experience, and he felt like a monk in his ‘chamber’.

But this achievement is nothing compared to that of Rajan Mahdeven who in 1983 set a new world recorded by reciting pi to its 31,811 digit. Rajan is just as adept at remembering other numerical information such as; License plate numbers, timetables, numbers of football players. Yet his Uni roommates confessed he was ‘Very good at remembering phones numbers, but I have to tell him over and over how to work the VCR.”

But the greatest of the greats when it comes to memorizing Pi is 27-year-old Hiroyuki Goto. Hiroyuki recited Pi to 42,000 digits over 9 hours in February 1995.
