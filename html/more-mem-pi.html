Pi: Paradise for number lovers

More ways to memorize p

 

Using Music:

Michael Harty used a phonetic (or sound) code in which each consonant sound represents a number. These numbers represented a letter, and these letters when played together sound like words. Which form weird and surreal stories.

As an example 1 could represent t, 4 represent r, and  9 represent p.

Put together 149 and you get trp, which sounds like turp as in turpentine.

Micheal Harty has memorized over 7000 digits of pi by making up songs and remembering his songs.

Here’s a snippet of one of his songs; it starts from 3.141…

 

[the 3 is] crying on a tulip, which moves away and picks up a device which puts a big notch into a yellow mule. The yellow mule becomes quite upset and he grabs a big box or laundry detergent call Fab . . . and he dumps it on a grey bomb and the grey bomb, of course, get angry as well . . .

 

Though this song is very weird they work effectively for him, and could for you as well.

 

Another word/poem mnemonic:

This poem mnemonic, was made up by F. Poyo.

It uses the mnemonic that a 1 letter word represents 1, 2 letter word represents 2, 7 letter word represents 7 and so on, and then stop when we get to zero.
